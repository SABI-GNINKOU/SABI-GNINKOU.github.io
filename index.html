About Me

Hello and welcome to my personal website! I am currently a PhD candidate in Applied Mathematics, specializing in Machine Learning, at Université Polytechnique Hauts-de-France (UPHF), in collaboration with École des Mines de Saint-Étienne (EMSE). My research focuses on the intersection of applied mathematics and machine learning, aiming to develop advanced probabilistic and algorithmic methods for modeling complex mechanical systems. Here, you can learn more about my academic journey, research projects, and professional background.

PhD Thesis Information

Topic: Gaussian Process Modeling of Mechanical Random Fields: A Comprehensive Study from Simulation to Identification

Supervisors: I am working under the supervision of Yacouba Boubacar Maïnassara, Rodolphe Le Riche, Andres Lopez-Lopera, Franck Massa, and Lucas Reding.

Funding: This project is supported by the ANR JCJC GAME program.

Institution: My research is conducted at Université Polytechnique Hauts-de-France (UPHF), within the Céramaths Laboratory, in collaboration with École des Mines de Saint-Étienne (EMSE).

Research Interests

  • Bayesian Inference and MCMC Methods
  • Machine Learning and Deep Learning
  • Variational Inference
  • Probabilistic modeling of mechanical random fields
  • Simulation and structural identification
